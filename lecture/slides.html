The Problem
Textbooks Are Static.
Learners Are Not.
  • Traditional materials are fixed — same content, same sequence, same depth for every student
  • Modern learners need personalised pacing, immediate feedback, and adaptive pathways
  • Educators know this — but have no accessible tools to act on it
  • The result: disengagement, knowledge gaps, and a "one-size-fits-none" experience
📚
Most textbooks in use today predate generative AI — and were never designed for personalised learning
The Core Paradox

AI can now generate bespoke learning experiences — yet 95% of classroom content remains static PDFs and printed books.

Why the Gap Persists
Two Barriers Educators Face:
Technical and Structural
Barrier 1 — Technical
  • Building AI tools requires API integrations, prompt engineering, hosting infrastructure
  • Ed-tech platforms offer generic AI — not tutors that understand your specific course material
  • Most educators cannot bridge this gap without developer support
Barrier 2 — Structural (the Black Box Problem)
  • Commercial platforms like Poe, ChatGPT give no access to conversation data
  • Teachers cannot see what students discussed with the AI or where they struggled
  • Without data, there is no feedback loop — AI tutoring becomes a one-way, disposable tool
  • Currently: teachers ask students to screenshot chat logs manually
Our Argument

Both barriers must be solved together. A no-code tool that still hides the data solves half the problem.

Theoretical Foundation
AI Is an Imitation
of Human Intellectual Behaviour

If AI fundamentally imitates human intellectual behaviour, then the correct design sequence is:

1️⃣
Observe how the expert teacher actually teaches — what sequence, what feedback, what judgements
↓
2️⃣
Encode that pedagogical method into the AI — not just content knowledge, but teaching logic
↓
3️⃣
Deploy at scale — the AI becomes the teacher's intellectual method, not a generic chatbot
What Most Ed-Tech Gets Wrong
  • Starts with AI capability, then asks how to fit it into education
  • Results in generic AI assistants with no domain pedagogy
  • The educator becomes a passive consumer
Our Reversal

Start with the teacher's method. The AI's job is to execute that method — faithfully, at scale, and with every student simultaneously.

Case Study — Natural Language Orchestration
Teaching Word Choice:
Encoding a Pedagogy in Plain Text
The Teaching Method (Human Version)
📋Diagnose understanding — MC questions, one at a time, with feedback before moving on
↓
🔍Guided practice — check individual words & combinations on demand
↓
✍️Real-context application — review word choices in the student's own paragraph
The Result

A structured feedback template {verdict · explanation · confidence %} designed by the teacher, executed by AI, received by every student simultaneously.

The System Prompt (Excerpt)
// Role definition You are an experienced lecturer in English. Your job is to help students improve their word choice in writing. // Workflow control When user types *menu*, present options ONLY — no extra information. // Pedagogical sequence Option 1 → diagnose (MC, one at a time) Option 2 → check individual word Option 3 → validate word combination Option 4 → review full paragraph

This is not vibe coding (which generates code via natural language).
This is natural language orchestration — directing AI behaviour directly, without any code generation step.

Priority Order (When Data Arrives)

First: Improve the AI prompt itself — if the tutor gave poor explanations, fix the system
Then: Identify individual students — who struggled? Who is ready for the next task?

Why This Matters

  • With data, it becomes an iterative teaching system that improves every cycle
  • The teacher becomes a system designer, not just a content provider
