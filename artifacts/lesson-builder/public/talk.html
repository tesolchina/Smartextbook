Where This Started
A Question Asked in
August 2024
"Where to start with generative AI chatbot customisation?" — Simon Wang, Times Higher Education Campus, August 2024
  • The article explored how educators could customise AI chatbots for their own teaching — without coding
  • At the time, the best available tool was Poe.com — a platform for building and sharing custom chatbots
  • The idea worked. Students engaged. But a critical problem emerged.
The Problem Poe Couldn't Solve
  • Teachers could build a chatbot — but couldn't see what happened when students used it
  • All conversation data stayed inside Poe — the educator saw nothing
  • No logs. No patterns. No feedback loop.
  • The chatbot was a black box — used once, then forgotten
The Question That Drove This Work

What if educators could customise AI tutors and own the conversation data their students produce?

⚠ The Catch

Google's result required a large engineering team. A language teacher at HKBU — or anywhere — cannot replicate it alone. The gap between research promise and classroom reality is not about the technology. It is about who has been empowered to use it.

Why the Gap Persists
Two Barriers Educators Face:
Technical and Structural
Barrier 1 — Technical
  • Building AI tools requires API integrations, prompt engineering, hosting infrastructure
  • Ed-tech platforms offer generic AI — not tutors that understand your specific course material
  • Most educators cannot bridge this gap without developer support
Barrier 2 — Structural (the Black Box Problem)
  • Commercial platforms like Poe, ChatGPT give no access to conversation data
  • Teachers cannot see what students discussed with the AI or where they struggled
  • Without data, there is no feedback loop — AI tutoring becomes a one-way, disposable tool
  • Currently: teachers ask students to screenshot chat logs manually
Our Argument

Both barriers must be solved together. A no-code tool that still hides the data solves half the problem.

Theoretical Foundation
AI Is an Imitation
of Human Intellectual Behaviour

If AI fundamentally imitates human intellectual behaviour, then the correct design sequence is:

1️⃣
Observe how the expert teacher actually teaches — what sequence, what feedback, what judgements
↓
2️⃣
Encode that pedagogical method into the AI — not just content knowledge, but teaching logic
↓
3️⃣
Deploy at scale — the AI becomes the teacher's intellectual method, not a generic chatbot
What Most Ed-Tech Gets Wrong
  • Starts with AI capability, then asks how to fit it into education
  • Results in generic AI assistants with no domain pedagogy
  • The educator becomes a passive consumer
Our Reversal

Start with the teacher's method. The AI's job is to execute that method — faithfully, at scale, and with every student simultaneously.

Case Study — poe.com/wordchoice
Teaching Word Choice:
A Full Pedagogy in Plain Text
Student Menu (type *menu* to begin)
1Learn about word choice & collocation — 3 MC questions, one at a time, with feedback
2Check collocation of an individual word — lists appropriate & inappropriate combinations
3Check if a word combination is appropriate — verdict + better alternative if needed
4Review word choices in a full paragraph — sentence by sentence, structured feedback
Structured Feedback Template (Option 4)

word combination: word 1 (pos) + word 2 (pos)
verdict: appropriate / not appropriate
explanation & example: …
confidence level: 10%–100%

The System Prompt (Excerpt)
// Role You are an experienced lecturer in English. Your job is to help students improve their word choice in writing. // Workflow trigger When user types *menu*, present the options ONLY — no extra information. // Option 4 — paragraph review Check one sentence at a time. For each verb, check its object. Use the structured feedback template: verdict · explanation · confidence % // Safety reminder (every response) Always remind: "I am powered by AI and can make mistakes — consult your human teacher."

This is not vibe coding — no code is generated.
This is natural language orchestration: plain English directing AI behaviour directly.

From Poe to LessonBuilder
The Evolution:
Same Idea, Solved Problems
2024 — Poe.com Approach
  • Write a system prompt → deploy a custom chatbot
  • Students access via a shared link
  • ✗ No access to student conversation data
  • ✗ Data owned by the platform, not the educator
  • ✗ No feedback loop — chatbot is a one-shot tool
The Insight

The customisation idea was right. The infrastructure was wrong. What if we kept the no-code simplicity — and built the missing data layer ourselves?

2026 — LessonBuilder
  • Paste any chapter → one teaching instruction → full lesson package generated in seconds
  • Lesson includes: summary · glossary · quiz · mind map · AI tutor
  • ✓ All student conversations stored and owned by educator
  • ✓ BYOK — use your own API key, zero platform cost
  • ✓ Open source — fully transparent, self-hostable
