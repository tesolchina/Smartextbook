## 1. Rationale, Purpose and Expected Outcomes (~200 words)

### Problem Statement
Hong Kong higher education faces a critical challenge: the growing gap between academic learning and practical job market preparation, compounded by insufficient personalized career guidance. With typical teacher-to-student ratios preventing individualized CV review and interview coaching, many students graduate with strong theoretical knowledge but weak job application skills.

*[Note in doc: "This section requires more input about the course as well as authentic stories about career coaching"]*

### Proposed Solution
This project develops an AI-enhanced career coaching partnership that amplifies teacher expertise through intelligent automation. Rather than replacing human guidance, our AI tools serve as "teacher clones" — replicating personalized feedback patterns to provide scalable, high-quality career preparation support.

### Core Activities
- AI-Powered CV Assessment: Automated evaluation using teacher-trained rubrics
- Interactive Interview Coaching: Chatbot simulating realistic interview scenarios
- Iterative Improvement Cycles: Students receive immediate, actionable feedback

### Expected Outcomes
- 25–35% improvement in CV quality scores (pre/post assessment using standardized rubrics)
- Enhanced interview readiness measured through structured evaluation frameworks
- Scalable coaching model serving 50+ students with teacher-level personalization
- Documented best practices for AI-human partnership in career education

## 2. Partnership — The Trio Model (~200 words)

### Student Partners (2–4 members)
Primary Role: Feedback Partners and End Users
- Content Contributors: Provide CV samples, interview audio recordings, and learning preferences
- Quality Validators: Test AI responses and report accuracy/relevance issues
- Peer Coaches: Support fellow students through structured feedback sessions
- Data Generators: Create real-world use cases and improvement scenarios

### Teacher Partners (1–2 members)
Primary Role: Content Creators and Quality Assurance
- Expert Knowledge Capture: Document feedback patterns, common CV issues, and interview best practices
- AI Trainer: Develop rubrics, prompt engineering, and response quality standards
- Pedagogical Designer: Structure learning progressions and skill development pathways
- Human Oversight: Monitor AI accuracy and intervene when personalized attention is needed

### GenAI Tools (ChatGPT-4, Claude)
Primary Role: Scalable Coaching Delivery
- CV Assessment Engine: Automated scoring using teacher-developed rubrics
- Interview Practice Bot: Scenario-based conversation partner with role-specific questioning
- Progress Analytics: Track improvement patterns and identify areas needing human intervention

### Partnership Philosophy
"Teacher amplification" model — AI extends rather than replaces human expertise.

## 3. Implementation Plan (September–December 2026)

### Phase 1: Foundation Building (September)
- Teachers provide direct CV review and interview coaching to initial cohort (10–15 students)
- Document all feedback patterns, common issues, and successful intervention strategies
- Students participate in peer review sessions
- Establish baseline CV quality scores

### Phase 2: AI Development & Training (October)
- Convert documented feedback patterns into AI training prompts and rubrics
- Develop AI-assisted CV assessment module
- Create interview simulation scenarios based on real industry practices
- Beta test with teacher oversight and refinement

### Phase 3: Pilot Implementation (November)
- Deploy AI coaching tools to expanded group (30–40 students)
- Students use AI for initial CV drafting and interview practice
- Teachers provide QA and handle complex cases
- Collect UX data and technical performance metrics

### Phase 4: Evaluation & Refinement (December)
- Comprehensive pre/post CV quality analysis
- Student satisfaction and learning outcome data
- Refine AI responses based on feedback
- Document scalability potential

## 4. Evaluation Methods (~200 words)

### Quantitative
- CV Quality: Pre/post rubric scores (content relevance, professional presentation, achievement articulation, industry alignment)
- AI Consistency: Inter-rater reliability between AI and teacher evaluation
- Interview Performance: Existing course rubrics for communication, professional presentation, content quality
- Self-reported confidence levels

### Qualitative
- User Engagement Analytics: adoption rates, session completion, feature utilization
- Student Satisfaction Surveys
- Teacher Workload Analysis
- Partnership Effectiveness assessment
- Cross-institutional comparison (HKUST + HKBU populations)

## 5. Ensuring Success (~200 words)

| Challenge | Solution |
|---|---|
| AI assessment validity and bias | Multi-layered validation, diverse training data from both institutions, continuous feedback loops |
| Maintaining AI response quality | Clear rubrics and templates, regular audits, teacher intervention protocols |
| Student data privacy (CV info) | Data anonymization, informed consent, GDPR compliance, secure institutional servers |
| Over-reliance on AI feedback | Built-in human touchpoints, peer collaboration, frame AI as enhancement not replacement |
| Resource requirements for maintenance | Standardized training protocols, comprehensive documentation, inter-institutional sharing |
| Cross-institutional collaboration complexity | Clear communication protocols, shared PM systems, regular review meetings |
