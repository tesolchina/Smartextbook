Session Outline: 

As AI becomes embedded in creative education, traditional assessment models centred on final outputs are increasingly insufficient. This seminar examines how arts-based, practice-led pedagogy can transition from passive consumption to participatory learning by emphasising process, experimentation, iteration, and reflective decision-making. It considers how AI can be used not to shortcut creativity, but to deepen artistic inquiry and support active engagement in the making process. By rethinking assessment to value process alongside product, the session invites educators to cultivate more authentic, critically engaged, and industry-relevant learning experiences in the age of AI.


Session Chair: Dr Suba Rajan 


Session Abstracts:

Karina Yuen will explore how educators can use accessible AI tools, such as NotebookLM and Gemini Gems, to customise AI chatbots that support students in three practical ways: by providing personalised guidance grounded in course materials, serving as conversational practice partners, and offering structured feedback based on instructor-defined criteria. Drawing on her work with faculty at NUS, Karina Yuen will share design ideas, practical tips, and approachable ways to get started. Participants will leave with concrete examples they can adapt to their own teaching context.


Simon Wang will explore "Democratising AI-Augmented Textbook Creation Through No-Code Chatbot Customisation". Traditional educational materials remain static and fail to provide personalised learning experiences that modern students need. While Google's "Learn Your Way" research demonstrated 11% learning improvements through AI-augmented textbooks, such innovations remain inaccessible due to technical barriers. We develop a platform that democratises AI-enhanced education by enabling educators to transform static content into intelligent, interactive experiences without programming knowledge. Using "vibe coding"—natural language instructions generating educational tools—educators create content-aware AI tutors understanding specific course materials. 



Damaris Carlisle will present the Theoretical Assessment Working Group (TAWG) findings, which advocate for a systemic shift toward process-oriented pedagogy and technological literacy. The session introduces a scaffolded assessment framework featuring process-oriented written assessment models to advanced oral defenses designed to prioritise original student thought and "human nuance" over generic AI outputs.


Brief Biographical Notes:

Karina Yuen is an EdTech Development Specialist at the AI Centre for Educational Technologies (AICET), National University of Singapore. Her work focuses on designing AI-enabled learning experiences that bridge pedagogy and technology, particularly through the use of educational chatbots in teaching and learning. She has conducted prompt engineering workshops for educators across universities and schools in Singapore and internationally. Before joining NUS, she led the institute-wide graduate teaching assistant training programme at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, where she began exploring how AI could support student learning at scale. Karina holds a Master of Science in Information Technology in Education and is a Gemini Certified Educator. Her work has been recognised with the Gold Award at eLearning Forum Asia 2024 and the Silver Award at the QS Reimagine Education Awards 2024.


Simon Wang is a lecturer in English and Innovation Officer at the Language Centre, Hong Kong Baptist University. He is committed to ensuring equitable access to the state-of-the-art AI technologies for the wide teaching and learning community. With his Ph.D. from the English Department of City University of Hong Kong, focusing on GRE writing test preparation, Simon is privileged to join the Language Center to help HKBU students develop their academic literacies and critical thinking skills in a world now shaped by digital technologies. His research interests include discourse analysis and computer-assisted language learning.
